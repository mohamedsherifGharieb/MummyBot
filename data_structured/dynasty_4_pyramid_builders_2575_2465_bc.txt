PHARAOH: Snefru
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sneferu
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the first pharaoh of the Fourth Dynasty of ancient Egypt, ruling around 2613–2589 BC during the Old Kingdom period.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Snefru introduced major innovations in pyramid design and construction, building at least three pyramids including the Bent Pyramid and the Red Pyramid at Dahshur, and the Meidum Pyramid. His work laid the foundation for his son Khufu's Great Pyramid.

AGE: Unknown, reigned for at least 27–30 years

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Built three surviving pyramids — more than any other pharaoh
- Pioneered the transition from step pyramids to true smooth-sided pyramids
- Laid the architectural foundation for the Great Pyramid of Giza

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was in the early Old Kingdom period under Snefru — stable, prosperous, and experiencing rapid growth in monumental architecture.

PHARAOH: Khufu
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khufu
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the second pharaoh of the Fourth Dynasty of Egypt, ruling during the 26th century BC. He is the pharaoh who commissioned the Great Pyramid of Giza.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Khufu commissioned the construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Very little else is documented about his reign beyond this monumental achievement.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Commissioned the Great Pyramid of Giza, the largest pyramid ever built
- The only confirmed portrait of him is a tiny ivory figurine found at Abydos

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was at the height of the Old Kingdom period during Khufu's reign — powerful, centralized, and capable of organizing the largest construction project in human history.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Djedefre
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djedefre
----------------------------------------

WHO: Djedefre was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh of the 4th Dynasty during the Old Kingdom. He was the son and direct successor of Khufu, the builder of the Great Pyramid.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Djedefre was the first pharaoh to use the title "Son of Ra," connecting Egyptian kingship directly to the sun god. He built his pyramid at Abu Rawash, north of Giza, though little of it survives today.

AGE: Unknown, reigned approximately 11 years

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- First pharaoh to use the title "Son of Ra" — a title used by all pharaohs after him
- Built his pyramid at Abu Rawash

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt remained stable and prosperous during the Old Kingdom under his short reign.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Khafre
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khafre
----------------------------------------

WHO: Khafre was the fourth king of the Fourth Dynasty during the Old Kingdom period (c. 2700–2200 BC). He was the son of Khufu and succeeded his brother Djedefre.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Khafre built the second-largest pyramid at Giza, standing next to his father's Great Pyramid. He is also credited by some Egyptologists with commissioning the Great Sphinx of Giza, though this remains debated.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Built the Pyramid of Khafre at Giza — second largest pyramid ever built
- Likely commissioned the Great Sphinx of Giza

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was in a period of great prosperity and monumental building during the Old Kingdom under his reign.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Menkaure
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menkaure
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was a king of the Fourth Dynasty of Egypt during the Old Kingdom, son of Khafre and grandson of Khufu. He completed the trio of great pyramids at Giza.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Menkaure built the third and smallest of the three famous Giza pyramids. He also commissioned famous statue triads showing him alongside the goddess Hathor and regional deities, which are among the finest sculptures from ancient Egypt.

AGE: Unknown, likely reigned approximately 18–22 years

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Built the Pyramid of Menkaure at Giza, completing the famous trio
- Commissioned exceptional statue triads now displayed in the Cairo Museum

EGYPT_ERA: The Old Kingdom was at its architectural peak during his reign, though there are signs of a gradual decline in royal power compared to Khufu and Khafre.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Shepseskaf
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shepseskaf
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the sixth and last ruler of the Fourth Dynasty during the Old Kingdom period. He likely reigned for approximately 4–7 years.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Shepseskaf completed the unfinished mortuary temple of his predecessor Menkaure using mudbricks. Unusually, he chose to build his own tomb as a mastaba (flat-roofed rectangular structure) rather than a pyramid — a significant break from tradition.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Completed Menkaure's mortuary complex
- Built the Mastabat al-Fir'aun at South Saqqara — the only royal mastaba of the Old Kingdom

EGYPT_ERA: The Fourth Dynasty ended with his short reign, with the Old Kingdom beginning to show signs of the shift in power away from the royal family.
