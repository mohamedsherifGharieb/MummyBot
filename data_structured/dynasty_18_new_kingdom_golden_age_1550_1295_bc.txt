PHARAOH: Ahmose I
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmose_I
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was a pharaoh who founded the Eighteenth Dynasty of Egypt. Ahmose restored Theban rule and reasserted Egyptian power, marking the beginning of the New Kingdom.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ahmose completed the conquest and expulsion of the Hyksos, rebuilt trade routes, and began massive construction projects that included the last pyramid built by native Egyptian rulers.

AGE: Approximately 35 years old at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, likely natural causes after ruling about 25 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Expelled the Hyksos from Egypt
- Restored Theban rule over all of Egypt
- Reasserted Egyptian power in Nubia and Canaan
- Began massive construction projects including the last native-built pyramid

EGYPT_ERA: During Ahmose I's reign, Egypt transitioned into the New Kingdom period, reunified after expelling foreign Hyksos rulers and beginning an era of unprecedented power and wealth.

PHARAOH: Hatshepsut
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatshepsut
----------------------------------------

WHO: Hatshepsut was the sixth pharaoh of Egypt's Eighteenth Dynasty, ruling from c. 1479 BC until her death around 1458 BC. She was Egypt's second confirmed female ruler and one of its most successful pharaohs.

WHAT_THEY_DID: She ruled as co-regent alongside Thutmose III, took on male pharaoh roles and appearance to legitimize her reign, and built some of Egypt's most iconic monuments including her Mortuary Temple at Deir el-Bahari.

AGE: Approximately 45–50 years old at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, likely natural causes around Year 22 of Thutmose III's reign (c. 1458 BC)

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Built the Mortuary Temple at Deir el-Bahari
- Oversaw construction at the Karnak Temple Complex
- Led a famous trade expedition to the land of Punt
- One of the most prolific builders in ancient Egyptian history

EGYPT_ERA: During Hatshepsut's rule, Egypt experienced a period of significant peace and prosperity — one of the most stable and wealthy periods in its history.

PHARAOH: Thutmose III
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thutmose_III
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the fifth pharaoh of the 18th Dynasty of Egypt. Thutmose III was regarded as one of the greatest warriors, military commanders, and strategists of all time — sometimes called the Napoleon of Egypt.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Thutmose III conducted between 17 and 20 military campaigns, all victorious, which brought ancient Egypt's empire to its largest size ever. He also created the ancient Egyptian navy, the first navy in the ancient world.

AGE: Approximately 56 years old at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Died of natural causes on 11 March 1425 BC after a reign of 54 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Conquered Canaan and Syria, expanding Egypt to its largest empire
- Created the first navy in the ancient world
- Won every single military campaign he led (17–20 victories)

EGYPT_ERA: During Thutmose III's rule, Egypt reached its absolute peak of territorial power and military dominance in the New Kingdom period.

PHARAOH: Amenhotep II
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amenhotep_II
----------------------------------------

WHO: Seventh pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty, ruled Egypt from approximately 1427 to 1401 BC. He was the son of Thutmose III.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Continued military campaigns in Syria and brought an end to hostilities with the rival kingdom of Mitanni. Known for his exceptional physical strength and athletic ability, which he documented himself.

AGE: 18 years old at accession; exact age at death unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Ended hostilities with Mitanni, bringing peace to the region
- Maintained Egypt's vast empire inherited from his father
- Documented as one of the most physically powerful pharaohs

EGYPT_ERA: During Amenhotep II's reign, Egypt enjoyed relative peace and prosperity, shifting focus from constant military campaigns to consolidation of its empire.

PHARAOH: Thutmose IV
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thutmose_IV
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was an Egyptian pharaoh during the 18th Dynasty, son of Amenhotep II and Tiaa. He ruled in the 14th century BC for approximately 10 years.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Thutmose IV is known for restoring the Great Sphinx of Giza and commissioning the Dream Stele to justify his claim to the throne. He established peaceful diplomatic relations with Mitanni by marrying a Mitannian princess.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died after a reign of approximately 9–10 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Restored the Great Sphinx of Giza
- Commissioned the Dream Stele
- Established peaceful relations with Mitanni through diplomacy
- Completed the tallest obelisk ever erected in Egypt at Karnak

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was prosperous and at peace during his short reign, with diplomatic expansion replacing military conquest.

PHARAOH: Amenhotep III
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amenhotep_III
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was one of ancient Egypt's greatest pharaohs, ruling from June 1386 to 1349 BC during the Eighteenth Dynasty. He is also one of few pharaohs worshipped as a deity during his own lifetime.

WHAT_THEY_DID: His reign marked a time of exceptional prosperity, grandeur, and artistic achievement. He commissioned major monuments including Luxor Temple and the Colossi of Memnon, and expanded Egypt's international diplomatic ties through strategic marriages.

AGE: Unknown, died in his 38th or 39th year of reign

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died after 38–39 years on the throne

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Commissioned Luxor Temple and the Colossi of Memnon
- Worshipped as a god during his own lifetime
- Expanded Egypt's international influence through diplomacy and royal marriages

EGYPT_ERA: During Amenhotep III's rule, Egypt reached the height of its artistic and international influence — the wealthiest and most culturally brilliant period of the New Kingdom.

PHARAOH: Akhenaten
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akhenaten
----------------------------------------

WHO: Pharaoh of ancient Egypt, tenth ruler of the Eighteenth Dynasty, originally named Amenhotep IV. He is known as the "heretic pharaoh" for abandoning Egypt's traditional religion.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Abandoned Egypt's polytheistic religion and introduced Atenism — worship of a single sun deity called the Aten. Built an entirely new capital city called Amarna and erased the names of other gods from monuments across Egypt.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died after approximately 17 years of reign

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Introduced Atenism, one of history's earliest experiments with monotheism
- Built the new capital city of Amarna from scratch
- Commissioned a unique and revolutionary style of Egyptian art

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt underwent a dramatic and controversial religious revolution during his reign, destabilizing the powerful priesthood and erasing centuries of tradition — changes that were reversed after his death.

PHARAOH: Nefertiti
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nefertiti
----------------------------------------

WHO: Nefertiti was the Great Royal Wife of Pharaoh Akhenaten and queen of the 18th Dynasty. She was a key figure in promoting Atenism alongside her husband during one of Egypt's wealthiest periods.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Nefertiti co-ruled with Akhenaten during the Amarna Period, overseeing the religious revolution and relocation of Egypt's capital to Amarna. Some scholars believe she briefly ruled as pharaoh under the name Neferneferuaten after Akhenaten's death.

AGE: Approximately 40 years old at death (c. 1370–1330 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Co-led Egypt's dramatic religious shift to Atenism
- One of the most powerful queens in Egyptian history
- Possibly ruled as pharaoh after her husband's death
- Her bust, now in Berlin, is one of the most famous works of ancient art

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was at its wealthiest during the Amarna Period, though the religious revolution created deep political instability that outlasted both Nefertiti and Akhenaten.

PHARAOH: Tutankhamun
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tutankhamun
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the thirteenth pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty, ruling ancient Egypt around 1332–1323 BC. Born as Tutankhaten, he is most famous for his intact tomb discovered in 1922.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Tutankhamun reversed Akhenaten's religious revolution, restoring Egypt's traditional polytheistic religion and moving the royal court from Amarna back to Memphis. He reestablished diplomatic relations with neighboring kingdoms.

AGE: Approximately 18 years old at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Died unexpectedly, likely from a combination of malaria and a leg fracture, around 1323 BC

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Restored Egypt's traditional polytheistic religion
- Moved the royal capital back from Amarna to Memphis
- His tomb, discovered in 1922 with over 5,000 artifacts, is the best-preserved royal tomb ever found

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was slowly recovering from the instability of the Amarna Period during his short reign, gradually restoring its traditional religious and political structures.

PHARAOH: Ay
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ay
----------------------------------------

WHO: Ay was the fourteenth pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty, ruling briefly from approximately 1327 to 1323 BC. He served as a high official and vizier under Tutankhamun before becoming pharaoh at an advanced age.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ay performed the burial rites for Tutankhamun and succeeded him as pharaoh. He continued the restoration of Egypt's traditional religion begun under Tutankhamun. His reign was very short, likely because he was already elderly when he took the throne.

AGE: Estimated to be in his 70s at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes after a very short reign of approximately 4 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Performed the burial rites for Tutankhamun
- Continued the religious restoration of traditional Egyptian worship

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was still in the recovery phase from the Amarna Period during his brief reign, with traditional religious practices being gradually re-established.

PHARAOH: Horemheb
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horemheb
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the last pharaoh of Egypt's 18th Dynasty, succeeding Ay. He was of common birth and rose through the military to become commander-in-chief before becoming pharaoh.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Horemheb reformed the Egyptian state, demolished monuments of Akhenaten, and systematically erased the Amarna Period rulers from official history. He authored the Edict of Horemheb to reduce government corruption.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died after a reign of at least 14 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Fully restored Egypt's traditional religion after the Amarna Period
- Authored the Edict of Horemheb — one of Egypt's earliest legal documents fighting corruption
- Reformed the military and state administration

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was fully stabilized and restored under Horemheb, closing the chapter on the Amarna disruption and setting the stage for the powerful 19th Dynasty.
