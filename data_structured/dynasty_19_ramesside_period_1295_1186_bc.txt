PHARAOH: Ramesses I
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramesses_I
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the founding pharaoh of Egypt's 19th Dynasty, succeeding Horemheb. He was of non-royal military origin and was already elderly when he became pharaoh.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ramesses I had a very short reign of approximately 1–2 years. He completed the second pylon at Karnak Temple and appointed his son Seti I as Crown Prince and heir.

AGE: Unknown, was elderly (estimated 60s or older) when crowned

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes after a very short reign of approximately 1–2 years. Buried in a small hastily finished tomb (KV16) in the Valley of the Kings.

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Founded the 19th Dynasty, one of Egypt's most powerful
- Completed the second pylon at Karnak Temple
- Established the succession of his son Seti I and grandson Ramesses II

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was transitioning from the stabilization of Horemheb's reign into the powerful and prosperous Ramesside era.

PHARAOH: Seti I
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seti_I
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the second pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty, son of Ramesses I. He ruled from approximately 1294 to 1279 BC and was considered one of Egypt's great warrior kings.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Seti re-established order in Egypt after the religious upheaval of Akhenaten's era. He fought the Hittites multiple times, reconquering disputed territories in Canaan and Syria. He built a magnificent temple at Abydos and the Mortuary Temple of Seti I at Qurna.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died after a reign of approximately 10–11 years

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Reconquered most of Egypt's disputed territories in Canaan and Syria
- Built the magnificent Abydos Temple with some of Egypt's finest relief carvings
- Built the Mortuary Temple of Seti I at Qurna

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was experiencing military expansion and cultural revival under his reign, re-establishing dominance over its neighbors and recovering prestige lost during the Amarna Period.

PHARAOH: Ramesses II
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramesses_II
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the third pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty, commonly known as Ramesses the Great. He is widely considered the greatest, most powerful, and most celebrated pharaoh in Egyptian history.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ramesses II fought over 15 military campaigns, built enormous temples and cities including Abu Simbel and Pi-Ramesses, and signed the world's first known peace treaty with the Hittites after the Battle of Kadesh. He ruled for 66 years — the longest recorded reign of any pharaoh.

AGE: Approximately 90–91 years old at death

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes after a 66-year reign. Buried in KV7 in the Valley of the Kings; his mummy is now in the Cairo museum.

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Built the Abu Simbel temples, among the most iconic monuments in Egypt
- Signed history's first known peace treaty (Treaty of Kadesh) with the Hittites
- Built the city of Pi-Ramesses as Egypt's new capital
- Celebrated an unprecedented 13–14 Sed festival jubilees

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was at the absolute peak of its power, wealth, and international fame during Ramesses II's reign — the golden age of the Ramesside era.

PHARAOH: Merneptah
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merneptah
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the fourth pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty, thirteenth son of Ramesses II, and the first royal-born pharaoh since Tutankhamun. He reigned from 1213 to 1203 BC.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Merneptah led military campaigns defending Egypt from Libyan invasions and repelled attacks from the Sea Peoples. He is best known for the Merneptah Stele, which contains the first known written mention of Israel in any historical record.

AGE: Around 60 years old when he came to power; exact age at death unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Died on 2 May 1203 BC, cause unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Created the Merneptah Stele — the oldest known text to mention the name Israel
- Successfully defended Egypt against Libyan invasion and the Sea Peoples
- Maintained Egypt's stability after his father's extraordinarily long reign

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt remained stable under his rule, though the country was facing growing external threats from Libyan tribes and Sea Peoples that would intensify in later reigns.

PHARAOH: Seti II
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seti_II
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the fifth pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty, son of Merneptah. He reigned from approximately 1203 to 1197 BC during a period of dynastic instability.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Seti II spent much of his reign fighting a rival claimant named Amenmesse who seized control of Upper Egypt. He eventually defeated Amenmesse and erased his monuments, but his reign was short and troubled.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Died after a reign of approximately 6 years, cause unknown

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Defeated the rival king Amenmesse and restored legitimate rule
- Built his KV15 tomb in the Valley of the Kings

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was politically unstable during his reign, with competing claimants fighting for the throne — a sign of the decline that would end the 19th Dynasty.

PHARAOH: Tawosret
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twosret
----------------------------------------

WHO: Tawosret was the last pharaoh of Egypt's Nineteenth Dynasty and one of the few female pharaohs in Egyptian history. She ruled briefly after the death of Siptah, whose regent she had been.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Tawosret served as regent for the young pharaoh Siptah after her husband Seti II died. After Siptah's death she became pharaoh in her own right, ruling for approximately 2–3 years before the dynasty ended.

AGE: Unknown

HOW_THEY_DIED: Unknown, died around 1188 BC

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- One of only a handful of female pharaohs in Egyptian history
- Built a memorial temple at Gournah
- Her tomb KV14 in the Valley of the Kings was later taken over by Setnakhte

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was in steep decline at the end of the 19th Dynasty — politically chaotic with short reigns, rival claimants, and weak central authority.
