DYNASTY FILE: ptolemaic_period_304_30_bc
============================================================

PHARAOH: Ptolemy I
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy_I_Soter
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was a Macedonian Greek general and close companion of Alexander the Great who founded the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt after Alexander's death. He ruled as pharaoh from 305/304 BC until his death in 282 BC.

WHAT_THEY_DID: After Alexander's death Ptolemy secured Egypt as his territory, retrieved Alexander's body and brought it to Egypt. He built the Library of Alexandria and the Lighthouse of Alexandria, and transformed Egypt into the leading center of Hellenistic culture.

AGE: Approximately 84–87 years old at death (born c. 369/368 BC, died January 282 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes at an advanced age of approximately 84–87 years old

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Founded the Ptolemaic Dynasty that ruled Egypt for 275 years
- Built the Library of Alexandria — the greatest library of the ancient world
- Commissioned the Lighthouse of Alexandria — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World

EGYPT_ERA: Under Ptolemy I, Egypt became a thriving center of Hellenistic civilization with Alexandria as its cultural capital, blending Greek and Egyptian traditions.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Ptolemy II
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy_II_Philadelphus
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the second pharaoh of the Ptolemaic Kingdom, son of Ptolemy I, ruling from 284 to 246 BC. His reign saw Alexandria reach the height of its cultural and intellectual brilliance.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ptolemy II promoted the Museum and Library of Alexandria to their greatest glory. He expanded Ptolemaic territory into parts of the Aegean and Levant through diplomatic and military means.

AGE: Approximately 63 years old at death (born 309 BC, died 246 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes at approximately 63 years old

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Elevated the Library and Museum of Alexandria to the height of ancient scholarship
- Expanded Ptolemaic power into the Aegean and Levant
- Commissioned the Septuagint — the first Greek translation of the Hebrew Bible

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt under Ptolemy II was at the cultural and intellectual peak of the Hellenistic world, wealthy and internationally influential.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Ptolemy III
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy_III_Euergetes
----------------------------------------

WHO: He was the third pharaoh of the Ptolemaic dynasty, ruling from 246 to 222 BC. His reign marked the height of Ptolemaic military and economic power.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Ptolemy III invaded the Seleucid Empire and won a near-total victory in the Third Syrian War, expanding Ptolemaic territory significantly. He also forged strong ties with the Egyptian priesthood through the Canopus Decree of 238 BC.

AGE: Approximately 58 years old at death (born c. 280 BC, died November/December 222 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes at approximately 58 years old

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Won the Third Syrian War, bringing the Ptolemaic Kingdom to its greatest territorial extent
- Issued the Canopus Decree, strengthening ties with Egyptian priests
- Continued expanding the Library of Alexandria

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was at its peak military and economic power under Ptolemy III — the most powerful the Ptolemaic Kingdom would ever be.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Cleopatra VII
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleopatra
----------------------------------------

WHO: Cleopatra VII was the last active pharaoh of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt, ruling from 51 to 30 BC. She was the only Ptolemaic ruler known to have learned the Egyptian language, as well as several others.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Cleopatra aligned herself with the most powerful Romans of her time — first Julius Caesar, then Mark Antony — to protect Egypt's independence. After Antony's defeat by Octavian (later Augustus Caesar), Egypt became a Roman province.

AGE: Approximately 39 years old at death (born 70/69 BC, died 30 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Died by suicide, likely by self-poisoning, on 10 or 12 August 30 BC to avoid being paraded as a prisoner in Rome

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Last pharaoh of ancient Egypt — her death ended 3,000 years of Egyptian rule
- Only Ptolemaic ruler to learn the Egyptian language
- Built powerful alliances with Julius Caesar and Mark Antony to protect Egypt

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was fighting for its survival against Roman domination during Cleopatra's reign, and ultimately became a Roman province after her death.

============================================================

PHARAOH: Arsinoe II
SOURCE:  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsinoe_II
----------------------------------------

WHO: Arsinoe II was the daughter of Ptolemy I and became co-ruler of Egypt alongside her brother and husband Ptolemy II Philadelphus. She was one of the most powerful women in the Ptolemaic dynasty.

WHAT_THEY_DID: Arsinoe II became co-ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom alongside her brother Ptolemy II after a turbulent earlier life that included marriages to a Thracian king and her half-brother. She was deified after her death and her cult became widespread across Ptolemaic territories.

AGE: Approximately 46–48 years old at death (born c. 316 BC, died between 270 and 268 BC)

HOW_THEY_DIED: Natural causes, exact circumstances unknown. Deified by her husband Ptolemy II after her death.

ACHIEVEMENTS:
- Co-ruler of Egypt with her brother Ptolemy II
- Deified after death — her religious cult spread across the Ptolemaic world
- Patron of scholars and known for her learning

EGYPT_ERA: Egypt was at a high point of Hellenistic prosperity and cultural achievement under the rule of Ptolemy II, with Arsinoe playing a key political and religious role.
